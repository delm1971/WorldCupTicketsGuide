The Current Buying Paths: Direct vs. Resale

If you are looking for seats today, you have two primary official channels, plus verified commercial secondary platforms:

1. Official Direct Last-Minute Sales Phase

FIFA’s official direct sales portal remains open throughout the entire tournament on a first-come, first-served basis. Because inventory is updated continuously up until game day as internal federation holdbacks are released, minor batches of face-value seats appear unpredictably. Checking this portal periodically is highly recommended, though wait times can stretch for hours during sudden drops.

2. Official FIFA Resale/Exchange Marketplace

The official, secure peer-to-peer marketplace remains fully active and runs until exactly one hour prior to each match's kickoff. Tickets bought here are automatically reissued with brand new mobile barcodes directly inside the buyer's tournament wallet, eliminating any chance of fraud. Note that FIFA implements a strict 15% transaction fee on both the buying and selling sides of this platform.

3. Commercial Secondary Aggregators

When official direct avenues are completely sold out, major resale hubs provide the widest available seat selection across all 104 matches. While these secondary networks offer multi-tiered buyer guarantees, they are subject to open market forces, meaning premium matches command steep demand premiums.
